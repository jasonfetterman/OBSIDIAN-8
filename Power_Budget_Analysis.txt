------------------------------------------------------------
1. PURPOSE
------------------------------------------------------------

Define quantitative power consumption model for OBSIDIAN-8 V3.

This document establishes:

- Peak current draw
- Average current draw
- Energy capacity modeling
- Mission duration estimates
- Safety energy margins

All energy-aware navigation logic must align with this model.

------------------------------------------------------------
2. BATTERY CONFIGURATION ASSUMPTION
------------------------------------------------------------

Example Baseline Configuration:

Chemistry: LiFePO4  
Nominal Voltage: 25.6 V (8S)  
Capacity: 20 Ah  
Total Energy:  

Energy (Wh) = Voltage × Capacity  
Energy = 25.6 V × 20 Ah = 512 Wh  

Usable energy (80% safety window):  

512 Wh × 0.80 = 409.6 Wh usable

------------------------------------------------------------
3. SUBSYSTEM POWER DRAW
------------------------------------------------------------

3.1 Servo Actuators

Idle current per servo: 0.2 A  
Active walking average per servo: 1.5 A  
Peak torque per servo: 4.0 A  

Assume 12 servos.

Idle total:
0.2 A × 12 = 2.4 A

Active walking average:
1.5 A × 12 = 18 A

Peak load:
4.0 A × 12 = 48 A

------------------------------------------------------------

3.2 Compute Stack

Main compute: 3.0 A @ 5V → 15 W  
Reactive MCU: 0.5 A @ 5V → 2.5 W  
Sensors (camera + depth + IMU): 10 W  

Total compute power ≈ 27.5 W  

At 25.6 V system rail:  
27.5 W ÷ 25.6 V ≈ 1.07 A

------------------------------------------------------------

3.3 Auxiliary Systems

Wireless communication: 5 W  
Cooling fans (if active): 5 W  

Total auxiliary ≈ 10 W  
Current at 25.6 V ≈ 0.39 A

------------------------------------------------------------
4. TOTAL CURRENT DRAW ESTIMATION
------------------------------------------------------------

Idle (standing):

Servo idle: 2.4 A  
Compute: 1.07 A  
Auxiliary: 0.39 A  

Total idle ≈ 3.86 A  

------------------------------------------------------------

Walking (average terrain):

Servo average: 18 A  
Compute: 1.07 A  
Auxiliary: 0.39 A  

Total walking ≈ 19.46 A  

------------------------------------------------------------

Peak dynamic maneuver:

Servo peak: 48 A  
Compute: 1.07 A  
Auxiliary: 0.39 A  

Total peak ≈ 49.46 A  

BMS and wiring must support ≥ 50 A continuous margin.

------------------------------------------------------------
5. MISSION DURATION ESTIMATION
------------------------------------------------------------

Using usable energy: 409.6 Wh  

Average walking power:

Power = Voltage × Current  
Power = 25.6 V × 19.46 A ≈ 498 W  

Mission duration (continuous walking):

409.6 Wh ÷ 498 W ≈ 0.82 hours  

≈ 49 minutes continuous walking

------------------------------------------------------------

Idle mission duration:

Idle power ≈ 25.6 V × 3.86 A ≈ 99 W  

409.6 Wh ÷ 99 W ≈ 4.1 hours idle

------------------------------------------------------------
6. ENERGY SAFETY MARGINS
------------------------------------------------------------

Mandatory reserve margin: 20%

Mission planner must:

- Trigger reduced capability at 30% SOC
- Trigger return-to-base at 25% SOC
- Trigger critical stop at 15% SOC
- Never allow drop below 10% SOC during motion

Pre-BMS cutoff margin must be enforced.

------------------------------------------------------------
7. THERMAL POWER CONSIDERATION
------------------------------------------------------------

Peak power events generate:

Peak servo power:
25.6 V × 48 A ≈ 1228 W instantaneous

Thermal design must dissipate
sustained loads without exceeding:

Servo temperature limit
Battery temperature limit
Regulator temperature limit

------------------------------------------------------------
8. WIRING AND CONNECTOR REQUIREMENTS
------------------------------------------------------------

Continuous rating must exceed:

1.5 × maximum sustained current

Recommended continuous rating ≥ 75 A

Voltage drop under peak load must remain
within acceptable tolerance.

------------------------------------------------------------
9. RANGE ESTIMATION EXAMPLE
------------------------------------------------------------

If average walking speed = 0.5 m/s  

Distance per hour:
0.5 m/s × 3600 s = 1800 m/hour  

At 49 minutes continuous walking:

~1470 meters theoretical max range  

Range must subtract 20% reserve margin.

------------------------------------------------------------
10. DESIGN INTENT
------------------------------------------------------------

This power budget ensures:

- Energy-aware navigation remains grounded in physics
- Battery sizing matches mission expectations
- Wiring and BMS rated appropriately
- Thermal envelope not exceeded
- Brownout risk minimized

Energy modeling must remain synchronized
with real-world telemetry calibration.
