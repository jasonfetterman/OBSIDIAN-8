OBSIDIAN-8 V3
System_Requirements_Specification.txt
Revision: V3.2
Status: Authoritative

------------------------------------------------------------
1. PURPOSE
------------------------------------------------------------

Define formal, testable system-level requirements for OBSIDIAN-8 V3.

All architectural documents, test protocols, and validation
activities must trace back to these requirements.

Each requirement is uniquely identified.


------------------------------------------------------------
2. REQUIREMENT IDENTIFICATION FORMAT
------------------------------------------------------------

Format:

REQ-[CATEGORY]-[NUMBER]

Categories:

MECH  – Mechanical
ELEC  – Electrical
CTRL  – Control
SAFE  – Safety
NAV   – Navigation
ENER  – Energy
SW    – Software
COMM  – Communication
OPS   – Operational
LIFE  – Lifecycle


------------------------------------------------------------
3. MECHANICAL REQUIREMENTS
------------------------------------------------------------

REQ-MECH-001  
System shall maintain structural integrity under defined deployment load.

REQ-MECH-002  
System shall withstand torsional loading within stability envelope.

REQ-MECH-003  
Center of gravity shall remain within defined stability polygon during stance.

REQ-MECH-004  
Frame shall tolerate vibration profile defined in Testing domain.

REQ-MECH-005  
All load-bearing fasteners shall be torque-specified and documented.


------------------------------------------------------------
4. ELECTRICAL REQUIREMENTS
------------------------------------------------------------

REQ-ELEC-001  
System shall segment servo and compute power rails.

REQ-ELEC-002  
Battery Management System shall enforce overcurrent protection.

REQ-ELEC-003  
Battery Management System shall enforce undervoltage protection.

REQ-ELEC-004  
Kill-line shall disable servo rail within defined latency threshold.

REQ-ELEC-005  
System shall prevent reverse polarity connection.


------------------------------------------------------------
5. CONTROL REQUIREMENTS
------------------------------------------------------------

REQ-CTRL-001  
System shall implement layered control authority model.

REQ-CTRL-002  
Reactive layer shall override mission commands when safety violated.

REQ-CTRL-003  
Heartbeat timeout shall result in deterministic fallback state.

REQ-CTRL-004  
System shall log authority transitions.

REQ-CTRL-005  
No motion shall occur without validated reactive authority.


------------------------------------------------------------
6. SAFETY REQUIREMENTS
------------------------------------------------------------

REQ-SAFE-001  
Hardware emergency stop shall directly disable servo rail.

REQ-SAFE-002  
Watchdog shall independently monitor reactive and mission heartbeats.

REQ-SAFE-003  
System shall default to STOP upon authority ambiguity.

REQ-SAFE-004  
Software shall not override hardware kill-line.

REQ-SAFE-005  
System shall prevent operation below defined energy critical threshold.


------------------------------------------------------------
7. NAVIGATION REQUIREMENTS
------------------------------------------------------------

REQ-NAV-001  
System shall classify terrain before traversal.

REQ-NAV-002  
System shall refuse traversal of hazardous terrain class.

REQ-NAV-003  
Navigation shall incorporate energy-aware constraints.

REQ-NAV-004  
System shall perform controlled docking within tolerance.

REQ-NAV-005  
System shall maintain slope traversal limits defined in Deployment Profile.


------------------------------------------------------------
8. ENERGY REQUIREMENTS
------------------------------------------------------------

REQ-ENER-001  
System shall estimate state-of-charge using coulomb counting.

REQ-ENER-002  
System shall initiate mission termination at defined SOC threshold.

REQ-ENER-003  
System shall avoid BMS hard cutoff during active motion.

REQ-ENER-004  
System shall log battery temperature excursions.

REQ-ENER-005  
Charging shall not occur during active mission state.


------------------------------------------------------------
9. SOFTWARE REQUIREMENTS
------------------------------------------------------------

REQ-SW-001  
All motion commands shall include sequence identifier.

REQ-SW-002  
All safety-critical messages shall include timestamp.

REQ-SW-003  
System shall reject stale or malformed motion commands.

REQ-SW-004  
Message definitions shall be version-controlled.

REQ-SW-005  
System shall log critical faults persistently.


------------------------------------------------------------
10. COMMUNICATION REQUIREMENTS
------------------------------------------------------------

REQ-COMM-001  
Remote control channel shall be encrypted.

REQ-COMM-002  
Unauthorized command attempts shall be rejected and logged.

REQ-COMM-003  
Loss of remote link shall trigger defined fallback behavior.

REQ-COMM-004  
System shall display current authority layer externally.

REQ-COMM-005  
Swarm commands shall not override local safety authority.


------------------------------------------------------------
11. OPERATIONAL REQUIREMENTS
------------------------------------------------------------

REQ-OPS-001  
System shall operate within defined environmental envelope.

REQ-OPS-002  
System shall require pre-deployment checklist validation.

REQ-OPS-003  
System shall log mission duration and fault events.

REQ-OPS-004  
System shall require manual reset after emergency stop.

REQ-OPS-005  
Maintenance intervals shall be defined and enforced.


------------------------------------------------------------
12. LIFECYCLE REQUIREMENTS
------------------------------------------------------------

REQ-LIFE-001  
All production units shall be serialized.

REQ-LIFE-002  
Engineering changes shall require documented impact analysis.

REQ-LIFE-003  
Calibration results shall be stored per unit.

REQ-LIFE-004  
Decommissioned units shall be marked in fleet registry.

REQ-LIFE-005  
System documentation shall be version-controlled.


------------------------------------------------------------
13. VERIFICATION TRACEABILITY
------------------------------------------------------------

Each requirement must map to:

- Design document
- Test protocol
- Validation result

No requirement shall remain unverified prior to Phase 2 exit.


------------------------------------------------------------
14. DESIGN INTENT
------------------------------------------------------------

This specification establishes measurable, testable,
and enforceable system-level requirements.

Architecture, code, testing, and lifecycle processes
must remain traceable to these requirements.

Safety and deterministic behavior are mandatory.
