OBSIDIAN-8 V3
THERMAL MANAGEMENT SPECIFICATION v1
Based on: V3_LARGE_FORMAT_BASELINE
Status: HEAT CONTROL & DISSIPATION DEFINITION
Units: Metric

---------------------------------------
PURPOSE
---------------------------------------

Define thermal management strategy for:

- Motors
- ESCs
- Power distribution
- Batteries
- Flight electronics

Prevent thermal runaway,
component derating,
and structural heat damage.

---------------------------------------
THERMAL DESIGN PRINCIPLES
---------------------------------------

1. Use open-air convection wherever possible.
2. Avoid sealed enclosures without venting.
3. Separate heat-generating components from printed nylon.
4. Maintain airflow path through central core.

---------------------------------------
MOTOR THERMAL CONTROL
---------------------------------------

Motors mounted externally at ring perimeter.

Requirements:
- No printed parts directly contacting motor housing.
- Maintain 5–10 mm air gap to any CF nylon.
- Allow unobstructed radial airflow.

Do not shroud motors unless forced-air cooling provided.

---------------------------------------
ESC PLACEMENT
---------------------------------------

Preferred Location:
Near motors on vertical posts or radial braces.

Mounting:
Directly to aluminum (heat sink effect).

Avoid:
Mounting ESCs to printed parts without metal interface.

Minimum spacing between ESCs:
20 mm

---------------------------------------
BATTERY THERMAL CONTROL
---------------------------------------

Battery location:
Lower central core (< 50% frame height).

Mounting:
Secure but not fully encapsulated.

Provide:
Ventilation gap on at least two sides.

Do not:
Sandwich battery between insulating materials.

Temperature monitoring recommended.

---------------------------------------
ELECTRONICS DECK
---------------------------------------

Flight controller and signal electronics:

- Keep separated from ESC heat zones.
- Maintain 15 mm minimum spacing between high-power modules.
- Avoid enclosing deck in sealed housing.

Optional:
Perforated shield for debris protection.

---------------------------------------
STRUCTURAL HEAT PATHS
---------------------------------------

Aluminum members act as passive heat sinks.

Encourage:
ESC-to-aluminum contact where appropriate.

Do not rely on:
Printed nylon for heat transfer.

---------------------------------------
AIRFLOW MANAGEMENT
---------------------------------------

Central 300 mm clearance cylinder must remain open.

Do not obstruct vertical airflow path.

Wiring bundles must not block
core ventilation path.

---------------------------------------
OPERATING TEMPERATURE TARGETS
---------------------------------------

Motor surface:
< 90°C

ESC casing:
< 80°C

Battery:
< 60°C

Flight electronics:
< 70°C

---------------------------------------
THERMAL TEST PROCEDURE
---------------------------------------

1. Operate propulsion system under load.
2. Measure component temperatures.
3. Record peak temperature after 5-minute run.
4. Inspect printed parts for softening or creep.

---------------------------------------
FAILURE CONDITIONS
---------------------------------------

Redesign required if:

- Printed parts show heat deformation.
- ESC exceeds rated temp.
- Battery exceeds safe operating temp.
- Electronics performance degrades due to heat.
