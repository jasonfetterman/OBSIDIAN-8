OBSIDIAN-8 V3
PROPULSION MOUNT GEOMETRY v1
Based on: V3_LARGE_FORMAT_BASELINE
Status: MOTOR STRUCTURAL INTERFACE DEFINITION
Units: Millimeters

---------------------------------------
PURPOSE
---------------------------------------

Define structural motor mounting geometry
and thrust load transfer path.

Mount must:
- Transfer thrust into aluminum structure
- Avoid loading printed parts in primary tension
- Maintain symmetry

---------------------------------------
PROPULSION LAYOUT
---------------------------------------

Configuration:
8 motor positions

Angular spacing:
Aligned with vertical posts (45° increments)

Radial position:
Primary ring center radius (556.5 mm)

Motor axis:
Vertical (Z+)

---------------------------------------
THRUST LOAD ASSUMPTION
---------------------------------------

Assumed maximum total thrust:
≥ 2 × all-up mass

For 60 kg design mass:
Total thrust ≈ 1200 N

Per motor (8 motors):
≈ 150 N nominal
Design margin:
300 N per motor

---------------------------------------
LOAD PATH
---------------------------------------

Motor Mount
→ 3030 Vertical Post
→ Ring Node
→ Radial Bracing
→ Opposing structure

Primary thrust must not:
Pass through printed component alone.

---------------------------------------
MOTOR MOUNT PLATE
---------------------------------------

Mount Type:
Aluminum plate (5–8 mm thick)

Recommended Diameter:
Based on motor base pattern

Mount must:
Bolt directly to 3030 post
or reinforced bracket

Fasteners:
M6 or M8 Grade 10.9 minimum

---------------------------------------
MOUNTING RULES
---------------------------------------

- Minimum 4 fasteners per motor
- Bolt spacing ≥ 30 mm
- No cantilever-only printed adapters
- Aluminum-to-aluminum compression contact preferred

---------------------------------------
TORQUE RESISTANCE
---------------------------------------

Each motor produces torsional reaction.

Mount must resist:
Motor torque + thrust vector offset.

No visible twisting of vertical post
under manual torque test.

---------------------------------------
CLEARANCE ZONES
---------------------------------------

Maintain:

Minimum 50 mm internal buffer
from primary ring inward.

No interference with:

- Upper ring
- Cross bracing
- Core volume cylinder (300 mm)

---------------------------------------
VIBRATION CONSIDERATION
---------------------------------------

Motor mounts must be rigid.

Do NOT use soft isolation
between motor and structural mount.

Vibration isolation occurs at electronics layer,
not propulsion interface.

---------------------------------------
THERMAL CONSIDERATION
---------------------------------------

Motor heat must not:

Transfer directly into printed parts.

Maintain 5–10 mm air gap
between motor and CF nylon components.

---------------------------------------
ALIGNMENT TOLERANCE
---------------------------------------

Motor vertical alignment:
< 0.5° deviation

Radial symmetry:
±2 mm position tolerance

---------------------------------------
FAILURE CONDITIONS
---------------------------------------

Redesign required if:

- Visible flex under thrust simulation
- Post torsional twist exceeds spec
- Printed components deform under load
- Fasteners loosen after dynamic run
