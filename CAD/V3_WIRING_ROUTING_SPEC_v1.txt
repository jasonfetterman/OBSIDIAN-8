OBSIDIAN-8 V3
WIRING & ROUTING SPECIFICATION v1
Based on: V3_LARGE_FORMAT_BASELINE
Status: ELECTRICAL PATH CONTROL DEFINITION
Units: Millimeters

---------------------------------------
PURPOSE
---------------------------------------

Define controlled routing paths for:

- Motor power lines
- Main battery leads
- Signal wiring
- Sensor wiring
- Communication antennas

Prevent:
- EMI interference
- Mechanical abrasion
- CG imbalance
- Service difficulty

---------------------------------------
GENERAL RULES
---------------------------------------

No loose wiring spans.

All wiring must:
- Be mechanically secured
- Follow defined routing channels
- Avoid sharp edges
- Avoid load-bearing contact points

Minimum clearance from aluminum edge:
2 mm (with edge protection if closer)

---------------------------------------
POWER DISTRIBUTION PATH
---------------------------------------

Battery location:
Lower core region (< 50% frame height)

Main power leads:
Route vertically through central core volume.

Motor power leads:
Route along vertical posts.

Secure every:
80–120 mm with clamp or tie anchor.

No diagonal free-span runs allowed.

---------------------------------------
SIGNAL WIRING PATH
---------------------------------------

Signal wiring must:

- Remain ≥ 30 mm away from high-current lines
- Cross power lines at 90° only if unavoidable
- Be twisted where possible

Dedicated routing channel:
Opposite side of vertical post from motor power lines.

---------------------------------------
ANTENNA PLACEMENT
---------------------------------------

Mount height:
Above electronics deck if possible.

Minimum distance from motor wiring:
100 mm

Maintain clear air volume
around antenna elements.

---------------------------------------
WIRE STRAIN RELIEF
---------------------------------------

At every connection:

- Provide slack loop (20–40 mm)
- Anchor wire before connector

No connector should bear mechanical load.

---------------------------------------
PASS-THROUGH POINTS
---------------------------------------

All brace intersections must:

- Include cable clearance gap
OR
- Use printed cable guide

No wire pinched between:
2020 and 3030 members.

---------------------------------------
THERMAL CONSIDERATIONS
---------------------------------------

High-current wires must not:

Contact motor housing directly.

Maintain airflow clearance.

Do not bundle:
Signal and power lines tightly together.

---------------------------------------
SERVICEABILITY
---------------------------------------

Electronics deck removable
without cutting wire ties.

Primary battery removable
without removing structural bracing.

Label both ends of:

- Motor leads
- Sensor cables
- Power distribution lines

---------------------------------------
EMI CONTROL
---------------------------------------

Twist motor phase wires.

Keep ESCs close to motors if applicable.

Avoid running power leads
parallel to sensor lines for long spans.

---------------------------------------
FAILURE CONDITIONS
---------------------------------------

Redesign required if:

- EMI interference detected
- Wire insulation abrasion observed
- Excess wire vibration visible
- Connector stress or loosening detected
