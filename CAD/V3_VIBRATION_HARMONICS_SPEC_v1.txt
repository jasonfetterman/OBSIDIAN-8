OBSIDIAN-8 V3
VIBRATION & HARMONICS CONTROL SPEC v1
Based on: V3_LARGE_FORMAT_BASELINE
Status: STRUCTURAL DYNAMIC STABILITY DEFINITION
Units: Metric

---------------------------------------
PURPOSE
---------------------------------------

Define vibration control targets
for structural integrity, electronics protection,
and long-term fatigue prevention.

---------------------------------------
PRIMARY SOURCES OF VIBRATION
---------------------------------------

- Propulsion system imbalance
- Motor harmonics
- Structural resonance
- Landing impulse loads
- Cross-brace coupling modes

---------------------------------------
DESIGN OBJECTIVES
---------------------------------------

1. Avoid structural resonance within propulsion RPM band.
2. Prevent amplification at vertical posts.
3. Prevent ring “drum mode” flex.
4. Isolate electronics from high-frequency vibration.

---------------------------------------
TARGET FREQUENCY BEHAVIOR
---------------------------------------

Primary structural natural frequency:
> 20 Hz minimum (preferred > 30 Hz)

Reason:
Avoid coupling with propulsion harmonic band.

No large flexible spans without bracing.

---------------------------------------
RING STIFFNESS RULE
---------------------------------------

Primary 3030 ring must behave
as a closed rigid loop.

No hinge-like corner behavior allowed.

Corner blocks must:
- Transfer shear effectively
- Prevent rotational slip

---------------------------------------
VERTICAL POST CONTROL
---------------------------------------

Posts must not behave as tuning forks.

Mitigation methods:
- Cross bracing between posts
- Upper ring stabilization
- Avoid long unsupported spans

---------------------------------------
RADIAL BRACE FUNCTION
---------------------------------------

Radial 2020 braces must:
- Reduce ring torsional flex
- Prevent ovalization under dynamic load
- Tie central mass to outer structure

---------------------------------------
ELECTRONICS ISOLATION
---------------------------------------

Electronics deck must:

- Mount to secondary structure (2020)
- Use vibration damping standoffs if required
- Avoid rigid coupling to propulsion mount nodes

Preferred isolation:
Rubber or TPU isolators (moderate stiffness)

---------------------------------------
FASTENER CONTROL
---------------------------------------

All structural fasteners:
- Medium-strength threadlocker
- Re-torque after first dynamic test

Loose fasteners amplify vibration modes.

---------------------------------------
PRINTED PART LIMITS
---------------------------------------

CF Nylon components:
- Inspect after first dynamic run
- No creep under sustained vibration
- Replace if stress whitening observed

---------------------------------------
VALIDATION METHOD
---------------------------------------

1. Assemble full frame.
2. Simulate vibration manually (tap test).
3. Listen for ringing resonance.
4. Perform controlled dynamic run.
5. Monitor for:
   - Excess flex
   - Harmonic amplification
   - Audible resonance peaks

---------------------------------------
FATIGUE PREVENTION RULES
---------------------------------------

- No sharp internal corners (minimum 3 mm fillet)
- Avoid thin cantilevered printed arms
- Distribute loads across multiple fasteners
- Avoid stress concentration at ring joints

---------------------------------------
FAILURE CONDITIONS
---------------------------------------

Redesign required if:

- Persistent resonance detected
- Structural oscillation visible at idle
- Printed components creep or crack
- Ring exhibits measurable cyclic distortion
