Maintenance_Procedure_Manual.txt
Revision: V3.2
Status: Authoritative

------------------------------------------------------------
1. PURPOSE
------------------------------------------------------------

Define standardized maintenance procedures for OBSIDIAN-8 V3
to ensure continued operational safety, reliability,
and compliance with lifecycle requirements.

Maintenance is mandatory for continued deployment authorization.

------------------------------------------------------------
2. MAINTENANCE CLASSIFICATIONS
------------------------------------------------------------

Level 1 – Routine Inspection  
Level 2 – Preventive Maintenance  
Level 3 – Corrective Maintenance  
Level 4 – Major Component Replacement  

Only qualified personnel may perform Level 3 and 4.

------------------------------------------------------------
3. MAINTENANCE INTERVALS
------------------------------------------------------------

Every Deployment (Pre-Mission):

- Visual frame inspection
- Check harness routing
- Verify battery charge state
- Confirm firmware version
- Confirm kill-line functionality

------------------------------------------------------------

Every 10 Operating Hours:

- Inspect fastener torque
- Inspect servo mounts
- Inspect connectors
- Review fault logs

------------------------------------------------------------

Every 50 Operating Hours:

- Recalibrate IMU
- Recalibrate joint zero positions
- Inspect battery health metrics
- Inspect servo temperature logs

------------------------------------------------------------

Every 100 Operating Hours:

- Full structural inspection
- Harness continuity test
- Power distribution inspection
- Replace worn connectors if necessary

------------------------------------------------------------
4. ROUTINE INSPECTION PROCEDURE
------------------------------------------------------------

Step 1:
Power system off.

Step 2:
Inspect frame for cracks or deformation.

Step 3:
Inspect harness for abrasion or pinch points.

Step 4:
Check servo mounting security.

Step 5:
Verify kill-line test:

- Activate hardware E-stop
- Confirm servo rail de-energizes

No mission permitted if failure detected.

------------------------------------------------------------
5. BATTERY MAINTENANCE
------------------------------------------------------------

- Inspect for swelling
- Check BMS logs
- Verify cycle count
- Confirm charge voltage within spec

Battery must be replaced if:

- Capacity degraded below defined threshold
- Abnormal thermal behavior observed
- Physical damage present

------------------------------------------------------------
6. SERVO MAINTENANCE
------------------------------------------------------------

- Check for excessive noise
- Check for abnormal heat
- Verify joint angle limits
- Inspect torque performance

Replace servo if:

- Repeated fault codes logged
- Thermal threshold exceeded
- Position deviation beyond tolerance

------------------------------------------------------------
7. SOFTWARE MAINTENANCE
------------------------------------------------------------

Before updating firmware or software:

- Backup logs
- Record current version
- Validate release tag
- Perform post-update validation test

No field updates without configuration record update.

------------------------------------------------------------
8. CORRECTIVE MAINTENANCE
------------------------------------------------------------

If fault detected:

- Identify subsystem
- Consult FMEA and Risk Register
- Document root cause
- Replace or repair component
- Re-run Operational Test Plan subset

------------------------------------------------------------
9. RECORD KEEPING
------------------------------------------------------------

Each maintenance action must log:

- Date
- Technician name
- Serial number
- Operating hours
- Work performed
- Observed anomalies

Records retained in lifecycle database.

------------------------------------------------------------
10. SAFETY REQUIREMENTS
------------------------------------------------------------

During maintenance:

- Servo rail must remain disabled
- Battery must be electrically isolated
- ESD precautions required for electronics
- No maintenance under active mission state

------------------------------------------------------------
11. RETURN TO SERVICE PROCEDURE
------------------------------------------------------------

After maintenance:

- Perform power-on test
- Run motion validation
- Verify energy status
- Verify watchdog heartbeat
- Clear logged faults

Only after passing checks may unit be redeployed.

------------------------------------------------------------
12. DESIGN INTENT
------------------------------------------------------------

Maintenance procedures ensure:

- Continued structural integrity
- Electrical reliability
- Safe operation
- Compliance with lifecycle governance
- Preservation of safety architecture

Maintenance discipline prevents failure escalation.
